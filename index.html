Atari 2600

Technical Specifications

Introduction

The Atari 2600 was based on the MOS Technology 6507 chip, a version of the 6502 with a 13-bit address bus (i.e. 8 kbytes of memory), offering a maximum resolution of 160 × 192 pixels (NTSC), 128 colors, 128 bytes of RAM with 4 kbytes on the cartridges (64 kbytes via bank switching).

The design has undergone numerous transformations and revisions over its 14-year production history, from the original “heavy sixer” to the Atari 2600 Jr at the end.

The system also features numerous third-party controllers and peripherals.

Processors
  • The CPU is in charge of scribing the screen for each frame, leaving 25% of its time for gaming.
  • Main processor: MOS Technology 6507 at 1.19 MHz
  • Audio and video processor: Television Interface Adapter (TIA)
Graphics Mode
  • Memory is not a bitmap, but a 160-pixel wide line.
  • Playfield resolution: 40 x 192 pixels (NTSC). Uses a 20-pixel register that is mirrored or copied, from left to right, to reach the 40-pixel width.
  • Sprites: 5 moving objects of a single color per line:
    • Two 8 × 192 players (rectangular pixels with a ratio of 1:2, zoomable 2 or 4 times) with a fineness equivalent to 160 × 192 without zoom.
    • Two missiles of identical color line by line to their player.
    • One ball, line-by-line identical in color to the background color of the playing field.
  • Colors: 128 (16 colors with 8 levels of intensity each) in NTSC and 104 in PAL. The French SÉCAM model displayed only 8 colors; in fact, it recolored the B&W version of the game.
  • Sound: 2 channels of 1-bit monaural sound with 4-bit volume control.
RAM
  • RAM (within a Mos Technology RIOT chip) : 128 bytes (additional RAM can be included in game cartridges).
  • Mass memory (game cartridges) : 2 and 4 Kbytes (but theoretically unlimited expansion via bank switching).
Connectors
    Input Connectors (controlled by MOS RIOT) :
  • Two screwless DE-9 controller ports, for single-button joysticks, paddles, trackballs, driving controllers, 12-key keyboard controllers (0-9, # and *) and third-party controllers with additional functions.
  • Six switches (original version) : On/Off, TV Signal (B/W or Color), Difficulty for each player (called A and B), Select and Reset. With the exception of the power switch, games could (and did) assign other meanings to the switches. On later models, the difficulty switches were miniaturized and moved to the rear of the unit.
    Output Connectors (B/W or color video TV signal and sound signal sent via an RF output connector) :
  • B/W or color TV video signal and sound signal sent via RF modulator (NTSC, PAL or SECAM, depending on region; game cartridges are interchangeable between NTSC and PAL/SECAM machines, but this will result in erroneous or missing colors and often a rolling image).
  • 9V DC jack-type power connector, 500 mA (internally converted to 5V DC).
